BLOG

India Gate, a magnificent structure nestled in the heart of New Delhi, stands as a poignant tribute to the valor and sacrifice of Indian soldiers. Conceived as a war memorial by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it was completed in 1931, serving as a solemn reminder of the sacrifices made during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The names of over 13,000 soldiers, etched on its walls, immortalize their bravery and dedication to the nation, while the eternal flame beneath the arch symbolizes the undying spirit of their sacrifice. Surrounded by sprawling lawns and bustling with activity, India Gate has become more than just a monument; it's a living symbol of national pride and unity, where people from all walks of life come together to pay their respects and honor the legacy of those who gave their lives for the country.
